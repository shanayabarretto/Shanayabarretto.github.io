St. Joseph Secondary School

I just completed my first co-op at Imagine Communications. I learned so much this term, and not just in technical aspects. Being in the workspace (though remotely) allowed me to learn from other professionals by example, which I found to be incredibly beneficial. I witnessed my coworkers problem solve, and began to use their techniques when I ran into issues. I also learnt the importance of keeping your workspace and code organized and neat. I was only able to look at code others wrote and understand it enough to make additions because they left thorough documentation and heavily commented code behind. Finally, working in this team environment definitely allowed me to become a better communicator. Attending stand up meetings allowed me to practice speaking clearly and concisely and was something I became increasingly more comfortable with as the term progressed.

I also tried to involve myself in outside of work activities and joined the social comittee. As part of that team, I helped organize and host a few events like a trivia night and a board game night. I hope to continue being involved in similar initiatives in future positions.

On the technical side, I became more comfortable with many more frameworks and programming languages. I worked a lot with Jenkins, Terraform, and Ansible, and became familiar with groovy and YAML file syntax. I also used a linux machine as my primary dev box, and have become proficient using its command line. Moreover, I also learned to use tools like docker and kubernetes, which we used for deployments. Additionally, I spent some time experimenting with powershell cmdlets, and gained experience using both powershell and C#. Lastly, since it was my first time programming in a team environment, I also learned to use git, this knowledge is now something I utilize for all of my personal projects. It was certainly a lot to learn in a short time, but as a result, I find the prospect of learning a large amount of new things for future roles much less daunting.

My high school offered a co-op opportunity for all Grade 12 students, which was to be a PATH student, or Teaching Assistant, for a Grade 9 or 10 class. My favourite classes in high school were my computer engineering courses, so I decided to become a PATH student for the Grade 10 version of the course. I had a lot of fun making lesson plans, teaching my students about computer history in interesting ways, showing them the real world applications of the circuit boards we made, and more. We covered a range of topics including binary, circuit components, logic gates, basic programming principles, and I pushed them to put their knowledge to use by guiding them to design solar power alarm clock circuits on Tinkercad, creating a binary counter with MultiMedia Logic, programming robots using VEX resources, and completing other similar projects.

My passion for engineering really grew during this time, and it really helped me confirm that this was the field I wanted to enter. I also learned a lot myself through making each new lesson plan, it was an experience I am very glad to have had.
